Important: An Extension Extends Your Time to FILE — Not to PAY

This is the single most misunderstood thing about filing extensions: the extension is for the return, not the payment. If you owe $8,000 and don't pay by April 15, you'll owe approximately $400–$600 in additional interest and penalties by October 15 — on top of the balance. If you're filing an extension because you don't have the cash, call us. We can discuss installment options and estimated payment strategies before the deadline.

The Process

How our extension filing works.

1

Complete the request form

Fill out your personal information, filing status, and total already paid to the IRS through withholding or estimated payments. Incomplete forms cannot be filed.

2

Pay the $150 deposit

The deposit is applied to your tax filing balance when you return for your full return. An online invoice is sent via email.

3

We file and confirm

Once payment and completed request are received, we electronically file Form 4868 and email you a confirmation.

4

Schedule your full return

It is your responsibility to schedule an appointment to complete your full tax return before the October 15 extended deadline.
